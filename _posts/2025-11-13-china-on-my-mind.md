The decline of the German industry has dire implications for Germany itself but also directly for Europe. Germany is the prime contributor to the EU budget which is an engine for redistribution, investment, research and education, cultural initiatives all across European countries.
If you live in Europe, you must be familiar with these signs on public buildings being renovated (schools, museums, infrastructures...), "EU participated to the funding of this equipment".

If and as Germany's welfare state gets threatened, Germany will reduce their participation to the EU budget. All other EU countries are in the same situation as Germany relative to China by the way, so that is going to be a general movement.

In areas where EU investment matters (and name one where it doesn't?), we should brace for more difficult times.
